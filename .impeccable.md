## Design Context

### Users
Fuse serves partnership, ecosystem, channel, marketplace, agency, and creator-led growth teams that are trying to find the right partners, understand fit, and move from research to useful engagement. They are usually operating inside messy partner ecosystems where data is scattered across passive CRMs, spreadsheets, emails, LinkedIn, website searches, notes, and ad hoc AI tools.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel clear, practical, and unusually alive. It should read like a founder-led product with a strong point of view: simple language, deep understanding of partner work, and no borrowed enterprise CRM story.

### Aesthetic Direction
The launch website design system is not the broader platform Fuse token migration. For this website rebuild, use a Twenty x Juicebox x Amigo hybrid:

- Twenty provides the engineering foundation, large editorial hero rhythm, product preview choreography, and responsive section system.
- Juicebox provides the product interaction reference: prompt-first search, result cards/tables, Search/CRM/Agents-style flow, and simple pricing shape.
- Amigo provides the premium editorial framing: asymmetric product media, restrained CTAs, thin bordered stage, and confident whitespace.
- Midday informs whitespace discipline and pricing simplicity.
- Chronicle informs clean pricing, resources, and legal pages.

The actual implementation should be Option 2, Prompt-First Partner Search, with Option 3, Enterprise Editorial Infrastructure, as the framing layer. It should not look like a direct Juicebox clone.

### Design Principles
- Do not implement the broader platform Fuse tokens right now. Create or adjust launch website tokens in `packages/twenty-website-new` only.
- Design from the website token hierarchy first: CSS variables, theme aliases, typography, spacing, radius, component primitives, then sections.
- Preserve the Twenty-derived website architecture where it helps speed, responsiveness, accessibility, and Vercel deployability, but remove the Twenty CRM story.
- Make search the first product signal: the home hero should center a Company | Person prompt and a cached animated results demo that does not depend on live Exa/Websets for first paint.
- Keep the problem model correct: Frankenstack is the problem; Busywork, Credibility gap, and Fake AI are the consequences.
- Use simple, direct copy. Prefer concrete partner-led growth language over generic startup language.
- Ship a believable v1. Do not invent proof, integrations, customer claims, or testimonials before real material exists.
